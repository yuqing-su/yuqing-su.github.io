abstract: Despite recent improvements, particulate matter (PM<sub>2.5</sub>) concentrations remain excessive, and concurrent worsening of surface ozone (O<sub>3</sub>) pollution has further amplified dual-pollutant risks. This trend underscores the urgent need for more accurate, continuous, and integrated air quality monitoring. To address this challenge, we proposed GeoFusion-RT, a novel high-resolution, all-hour retrieval framework for PM<sub>2.5</sub> and O<sub>3</sub>, to generate seamless 24-hour pollutant concentration fields over East-Central China from 2017 to 2023. Model evaluations show strong predictive performance, with cross-validated coefficients of determination (R<sup>2</sup>) of 0.92 for PM<sub>2.5</sub> and 0.94 for O<sub>3</sub>, and root mean squared errors (RMSEs) of 8.59 μg/m<sup>3</sup> and 10.94 μg/m<sup>3</sup>, respectively. Independent site-based validation further confirms robust generalizability (PM<sub>2.5</sub>  R<sup>2</sup> = 0.86; O<sub>3</sub>  R<sup>2</sup> = 0.89), demonstrating high accuracy across diurnal, seasonal, and interannual timescales. Trend analysis reveals a reduction of over 10 μg/m<sup>3</sup> in annual PM<sub>2.5</sub> concentrations from 2017 to 2020, with particularly sharp declines in regions such as Beijing–Tianjin–Hebei and the Yangtze River Delta. However, this progress stagnated or slightly reversed after the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, possibly reflecting the saturation of earlier interventions or diminishing policy effectiveness. In contrast, O<sub>3</sub> showed limited decline prior to 2020 but has risen sharply since, with an annual increase of approximately +1.5 μg·m<sup>-3</sup>·yr<sup>-1</sup> by 2023, highlighting a decoupled trend between the two pollutants. These findings emphasize the urgency for coordinated precursor controls, targeting both NOx and VOCs, to prevent worsening O<sub>3</sub> pollution under PM<sub>2.5</sub>-only reduction pathways in the post-COVID era.
